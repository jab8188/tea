Specialty Tea



White Flower Tea

Made from infused dried herbs, fruits, and flowers; Popular varieties: Hibiscus Tea, Peppermint, Chamomile Tea, Yerba mate; while not actually made from tea, still included when talking about tea but in fact is considered a tisane and not a “true” tea; also known as Tisane (tee-zahn) – French term adopted by western cultures, for any infused beverage made with anything other than actual tea leaves (Ex: Cranberry Hibiscus Tea, Tuscan Garden Tea, Blueberry Acai Tea)



Rooibos Tea

Made from dried rooibos plant; partially oxidized; Popular Varieties: Red Rooibos, Green Rooibos; Particular type of herbal tea made from a plant native to South Africa; also referred to as red tea or red bush tea, naturally caffine free; Has a full body similar to that of black tea; natural sweetness



Wellness Blend

Sub-varieties and wellness blends like, Puerh (originated in the city of Pu-erh in the Yunnan province of China), Kukicha, Genmaicha; these teas run the gamut of flavors and character due to the conditions under which they are produced; the blending of particular teas has a long, rich history in western culture; (Ex: Relax, Inflammation, Detox, Throat Coat, so many more)



Purple Tea

Newer type of tea, has only been available for a few years; Produced from a rare purple-leaved tea plant; grows wild in the Assam region of India; Today, purple teas are primarily produced in Kenya, Africa; Have a light body and mellow flavor, extremely low n caffine and high in antioxidants and anthcyanins; Produced in a manner simiar to oolong teas; leaves are harvested, wilted, and undergo partial oxidation before being shaped and dried; when bewed, purple tea brews up a light reddish-purple; Originally found growing in Assam region of India, later taken to Kenya; now our purple teas come from the Tumoi Tea Garden in the Nandi Hills of Kenya
