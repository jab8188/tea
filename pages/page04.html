Oolong Tea



Another tea that is made from the Cameilia sinensis tea plant is Oolong tea. This tea is partially oxidized, compared to some of the other teas. Oolong sits between a green tea and a black tea on a color scale. The depth of character ranges greatly and is partially a result of the degree of oxidation. This is how Oolong tea can end up being closer to a green tea or a black tea, depending on how it is produced. The produced liquor can range from a greenish yellow to a dark amber color. One primary example is the Organic Jade Oolong tea. This tea is primarily produced in China and Taiwan.
